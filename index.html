Our Mission

At Second Step Tech, we believe in the power of technology to transform lives. Our mission is to collect, refurbish, and redistribute technology to underserved communities, organizations, and individuals who lack access to digital resources.

In today's increasingly digital world, access to technology is no longer a luxury but a necessity. From employment opportunities and healthcare access to everyday connectivity, technology plays a crucial role in daily life. Yet millions remain disconnected due to economic barriers.

We're committed to addressing this inequality through sustainable, community-focused solutions. By giving technology a second life, we're not only helping people connect to essential resources but also reducing e-waste and promoting environmental sustainability.

About Second Step Tech

Second Step Tech was founded with a simple idea: give used technology a second step in its lifecycle while helping bridge the digital divide. We collect donated devices, refurbish them, and distribute them to those in need.

Bridging the digital divide
Sustainable technology practices

Founded in 2024 by a group of passionate high school students, Second Step Tech began as a small initiative to help local community members access laptops for remote work and digital connectivity during the pandemic. What started as a neighborhood project quickly grew into a community-wide movement as we witnessed the overwhelming need for technology access across all demographics.

Today, we've expanded our reach to serve organizations, community centers, senior facilities, and individual families throughout the region. Our team of dedicated volunteers works tirelessly to collect, refurbish, and redistribute technology to those who need it most, while also providing technical support and digital literacy resources.

What We Do

  • Device Collection: We gather donated computers, laptops, phones, and tablets from individuals, organizations, and businesses. Our collection drives make it easy for donors to responsibly dispose of unwanted technology while making a positive impact.
  • Refurbishment & Repair: Our skilled volunteers and technicians wipe data, repair hardware issues, and install or update operating systems to ensure each device is ready for new use. We specialize in extending the useful life of technology that would otherwise end up in landfills.
  • Testing & Quality Assurance: Every device goes through a thorough inspection and testing process to confirm functionality and reliability. We ensure each recipient receives technology that meets their needs and works properly.
  • Distribution: We partner with community organizations and nonprofits to place these refurbished devices in the hands of those who will benefit the most. Our targeted distribution ensures technology reaches those with limited access and greatest need.
  • Digital Literacy Training: We provide basic training and resources to help recipients make the most of their new devices, covering essential skills from setup and maintenance to online safety and productivity applications.
  • Ongoing Support: We offer additional resources so recipients can maintain and maximize their refurbished devices. Our support team is available to troubleshoot issues and answer questions as they arise.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you ensure my data is removed from donated devices?

We take data security very seriously. All donated devices undergo a thorough data sanitization process using industry-standard software that meets Department of Defense requirements for data destruction. For devices with hard drives, we use multi-pass wiping techniques to ensure all personal information is completely removed. For devices that cannot be securely wiped, we physically remove and destroy the storage components before recycling the remaining hardware.

Are donations tax-deductible?

Yes, all donations to Second Step Tech are tax-deductible. We are a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, and we provide receipts for all contributions, including both device donations and monetary support. The receipt will include our tax ID number and a description of your donation for your records.

What happens to devices that can't be refurbished?

Devices that cannot be refurbished are responsibly recycled through our certified e-waste recycling partners. We ensure that no donated technology ends up in landfills and that all components are processed according to environmental best practices. In some cases, we may salvage working parts from non-functional devices to use in repairs of other equipment.

How do you decide who receives the refurbished devices?

We work with community organizations and social service agencies to identify individuals and groups with the greatest need for technology access. Recipients are selected based on factors including financial need, potential impact, and overall benefit to the community. We also maintain an application process for those who request assistance directly.

What kind of training do you provide with the devices?

Each recipient receives basic training on device setup, operation, and maintenance. Depending on their needs and experience, we may also provide instruction on internet basics, online safety, productivity software, and other essential digital skills. Our goal is to empower everyone to use their device confidently.

Can businesses donate in bulk?

Absolutely! We welcome corporate donations and can facilitate bulk pickups for organizations upgrading their technology. Corporate donations make a significant impact on our ability to serve the community, and we work with your IT department to ensure a smooth process. We also offer special recognition for our corporate partners and provide detailed documentation for sustainability reporting.

Apply for Technology

We're committed to providing technology to those who need it most. If you or someone you know could benefit from access to a refurbished device, please complete our application form below.

Who Can Apply

We prioritize providing technology to individuals and organizations that demonstrate both need and potential impact. Our recipients typically include:

  • Individuals without access to necessary technology for daily tasks
  • Job seekers needing devices for applications and skill development
  • Seniors requiring technology for healthcare management and family connection
  • Nonprofit organizations serving vulnerable populations
  • Community centers and programs in underserved areas
  • Families with financial hardship and limited technology access

What to Expect

Our application process is designed to be straightforward and respectful of your time and privacy:

  1. Submit Application: Complete the form below with information about your technology needs and situation.
  2. Review Period: Our team reviews applications on a monthly basis. We'll contact you within 2-4 weeks regarding your application status.
  3. Assessment: If your application moves forward, we may schedule a brief conversation to better understand your specific technology needs.
  4. Device Matching: Once approved, we'll match you with an appropriate device based on your requirements and our available inventory.
  5. Distribution: You'll be invited to pick up your device at our center, where you'll receive basic training on its use and maintenance.
  6. Follow-up Support: Our team remains available to provide technical assistance as you begin using your new device.

Additional Resources

Even if you don't qualify for our device program, we're committed to helping you access technology. Here are some alternative resources that may be helpful:

  • Public Libraries: Most libraries offer free computer and internet access, as well as digital literacy classes.
  • Community Centers: Many neighborhood centers provide technology access during regular hours.
  • Low-Cost Internet Programs: Several providers offer reduced-rate internet service for qualifying households.
  • Digital Literacy Workshops: We regularly host free workshops on basic computer skills and online safety. Check our events calendar for upcoming sessions.
  • Technology Assistance Hotline: Our volunteers staff a help line to answer basic technology questions and provide guidance, even if you're not a recipient of our devices.
